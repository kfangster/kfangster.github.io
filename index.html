The NeuroEngineering and Biomedical Instrumentation Lab study neuroprostheses with the central goal of improving the restoration of motor and somatosensory functions with upper-limb prostheses.   • Developed a prototype transhumeral bypass using Solidworks for experimental procedures

Mask-R-Aid (UCI Biodesign)

September 2020 - June 2021, Irvine, California

The COVID-19 pandemic mandated the use of face masks worldwide. Although they were essential to preventing the spread of the virus, face masks often caused its wearers discomfort for a multitude of reasons. An XPRIZE survey from summer of 2020 listed that the major complaints from the participants were that masks were too hot, made breathing difficult, fogged up glasses, or were uncomfortable. The goal of our device was to create a comfortable face mask that addressed these complaints while continuing to provide adequate protection against the virus. The novelty of our device will consist of a fan blower system, HEPA filters, and a transparent front portion.

Responsibilities:
  • Created multiple generation CAD models and basic engineering drawings of a novel PAPR Mask using Solidworks
  • Developed basic Design Control parameters to follow for electrically powered mask respirators
  • 3D printed these prototypes using both SLA and FLM printers with biocomptabile materials
